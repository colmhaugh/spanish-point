Welcome to Spanish Point


Spanish Point (Irish: Rinn na Spáinneach) is a small resort in the parish of Milltown Malbay in County Clare on the west coast of Ireland. It’s so named because of the Spaniards who were buried here after the wreck of their Armada ships along the coast in 1588. One of the ships was wrecked on the reefs to landward of Mutton Island (3km/2 miles) off shore. More than a thousand men were lost, and many of their bodies were carried by the tide to Spanish Point. Today, you can hire a currach in the fishing village of Quilty and visit the site of the wreck.


Spanish Point is located 3 km/2 miles west of Milltown Malbay, is a small resort with a beautiful sandy beach, lovely hotels and an award winning 9 hole golf course.

Spanish Point has a beautiful long sandy beach within a sheltered location makes it a family friendly shore, perfect for shelling and sandcastle-building. The beach is located on the northern side of the village and has ample parking. Its high waves rolling in from the Atlantic Ocean are popular with surfers as with swimmers alike, surfing schools operate here in summer. A number of picnic benches are dotted along the length of the beach for those of you that love to picnic at the seaside.
